IP Address

An Internet Protocol (IP) Address is a unique address that identifies a device on the internet or a local network.

Think of it like a House Address. To receive mail (data packets), you need a specific location that the sender can target.

  • IPv4: Four sets of numbers (e.g., 192.168.1.1) - most common.
  • IPv6: Longer, hexadecimal format to accommodate more devices.
